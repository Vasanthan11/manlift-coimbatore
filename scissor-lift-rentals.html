Selection guide

How to choose the right scissor lift

Instead of comparing long spec sheets, use these simple real-site factors to narrow down the right machine.

Working height
Exact reach matters

Measure the highest point you need to reach from finished floor level. Keep a small safety margin instead of working at maximum height.

Floor condition
Indoor vs outdoor

Smooth epoxy and tiles suit lifts. Uneven or unfinished ground calls for rough-terrain scissor lifts with better grip.

Platform load
People + material

Count everyone working on the platform plus tools, fixtures and materials. This helps avoid under-sizing the lift capacity.

Movement
Number of locations

If you must move frequently between bays or blocks, compact scissor lifts can save time and effort.

Duration
Short vs long term

Short shutdowns and projects may use daily or weekly rentals. Long-term jobs are usually better on monthly plans.

Site rules
Restrictions & permits

Noise curfews, emission rules, access windows and induction requirements can influence the final model choice.
